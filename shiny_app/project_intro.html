Purpose of Our Research

How do we become friends with the people we are friends with? Are the friends we make truly representative of our interests or are they actually determined by uncontrollable factors like the dorms we live in, our extracurriculars, our race, and where we come from? In seeking to answer this question and others like it, we mapped and analyzed the literal social network of the Harvard Class of 2024.

Jeremiah Kim conducted a very similar project last year, and building off of his team's findings, we were particularly interested to see how first-years were forming connections and staying connected with each other in this unprecedented time shaped by the COVID-19 pandemic. We were curious to see what significant differences might be noticable between this semester and the fall semester last year.

Collecting Data

We sent out a survey to members of the Harvard Class of 2024 (first-years) to gather the data for our project. We scraped emails from the Harvard face book to contact first-years and also made use of class Facebook and Instagram pages, Slack channels, GroupMe chats, and other social media forums to publicize the survey.

Survey Questions

Demographic Questions
  1. Have you taken a gap year?
  2. Where are you living this semester?
  3. If you are living on campus, are you in a single or do you have roommates/suitemates?
  4. Which gender identity do you most identify with?
  5. Specify your race and/or ethnicity.
  6. Which pre-orientation program, if any, did you participate in?
  7. Are you involved with any sports teams on campus?
Social Connection Questions
  1. In your opinion, which of the following is best for forming meaningful connections?
    • Zoom lecture
    • Breakout room of a class
    • Virtual entryway
    • Living in close proximity (same floor, same house, etc.)
    • Already knew them before coming to Harvard
    • Roommate/Suitemate
    • Friend of a friend
    • Pre-orientation program
    • Club/co-curricular activity
    • Donut/other randomized Slack bot
    • Social media (GroupMe, Instagram, Discord, etc.)
    • Virtual event to meet people (“speed friending” or similar)
    • Meet24
    • Classfinds
  2. In your opinion, which of the following social media platforms is the best for forming meaningful connections?
    • GroupMe
    • Instagram
    • Facebook
    • Snapchat
    • Discord
    • Slack
    • Patio
    • Texting
    • FaceTime/video call
  3. List the names of the four people you are closest friends with in the Class of 2024 and specify where/how you first met each of them and how close you feel to each of them.
  4. How do you stay in contact with these four people?
  5. How many people have you met virtually that you now often meet up with in-person?
  6. When you do meet with people in-person, where do you go or what do you do?
  7. Name the person in the Class of 2024 who you think is the most socially connected.
  8. How satisfied are you with your social connections with other first-year students at Harvard?

Respondents

Approximately 400 first-years filled out the survey. There are approximately 1600 students in a Harvard class, but it's important to note that this number would be lower for the Class of 2024 specifically because approximately 20% of the class decided to take a gap year. The increased number of students interested in gap years was presumably evidence of many graduating seniors' wanting to delay enrollement in the hopes of getting a more typical college experience in a year when things might be closer to the old normal.
